Off-Campus Apartment Hunting Tips for Students

Finding your first apartment as a student is overwhelming. Here's what experienced students wish they knew before signing a lease.

When to Start Looking

- Start 2-3 months before your move-in date in most markets
- In college towns, start even earlier (3-4 months) because student housing goes fast
- August/September move-ins: start looking in May/June
- January move-ins: start looking in October/November
- Tour places in person if at all possible - photos lie

Red Flags When Apartment Hunting

Watch out for:
- Landlords who won't let you see the actual unit (only a "model" unit)
- "Application fees" over $50 (some are scams collecting fees with no real unit)
- Lease clauses that make you responsible for ALL repairs
- No written lease (verbal agreements protect you from nothing)
- Pest issues: look in corners, under sinks, behind appliances during tours
- Water damage: check ceiling corners and under bathroom sinks
- Pressure to sign immediately ("someone else is interested") - legitimate places give you time
- Utilities not clearly defined in the lease (who pays what)
- "Furnished" apartments with broken/dirty furniture you'll be charged for later

Understanding Your Lease

Critical things to verify before signing:
- Exact monthly rent and when it's due
- Security deposit amount and conditions for getting it back
- Lease duration (12 months? 9 months for academic year?)
- Subletting policy (can you sublet over summer if you leave?)
- Guest policy (overnight guests, how many, how long)
- Pet policy (even if you don't have pets now)
- Maintenance request process and response time expectations
- Early termination clause and penalties
- Renewal terms (does rent increase? Do you go month-to-month?)
- Parking situation (included? Extra cost? Street parking only?)

Roommate Strategies for Saving Money

Living with roommates is the #1 way students save on housing:
- 1-bedroom solo: $1,200-1,800/month in most college towns
- 2-bedroom split: $600-900/month each
- 3-4 bedroom house split: $400-700/month each

Finding good roommates:
- University housing boards and Facebook groups
- Friends from classes (but set ground rules before signing)
- Roommate matching services (some universities offer these)

Roommate agreements should cover:
- How to split utilities (evenly? By usage? By room size?)
- Quiet hours and guest policies
- Cleaning responsibilities and schedules
- Shared vs. personal groceries/supplies
- What happens if someone wants to move out early
- Temperature preferences (heating/cooling battles are real)

Hidden Costs of Off-Campus Living

Budget for these beyond just rent:
- Security deposit (usually one month's rent, due before move-in)
- First + last month's rent (some landlords require both upfront)
- Utilities: electricity ($30-80), gas ($20-50), water ($20-40), internet ($40-60)
- Renter's insurance ($10-20/month - protects your stuff from theft/fire)
- Furniture if unfurnished (check Facebook Marketplace, university move-out days for free stuff)
- Kitchen basics: pots, pans, utensils, cleaning supplies ($100-200 initial investment)
- Laundry: if no in-unit washer, budget $20-40/month for laundromat
- Moving costs: truck rental, boxes, asking friends to help with pizza and beer

Move-In Checklist

Before you sign:
- Take photos/video of EVERYTHING during move-in walk-through
- Document any existing damage on the move-in condition form
- Test all faucets, toilets, stove burners, oven, outlets
- Check that all windows and doors lock properly
- Verify smoke detectors and carbon monoxide detectors work
- Note the location of the circuit breaker and water shutoff
- Get everything in writing - verbal promises from landlords mean nothing
- Save copies of all communication with your landlord

Emergency Contacts to Post on Your Fridge
- Landlord/property manager number
- Maintenance emergency line
- Poison control
- Non-emergency police line
- Nearest urgent care address
- Trusted neighbor's number
